## 1. Purpose

This document defines the documentation standards for the Travel MidOffice knowledge base.

The goal is to keep the handbook consistent, maintainable, and useful for Product, Engineering, Operations, Finance, QA, Management, and integration partners.

---

## 2. Document Principles

- Every document must answer one primary question.
- Business meaning comes before implementation detail.
- Confirmed architecture must be separated from current design and future consideration.
- Business rules must reference rule IDs from the Business Rules Catalog.
- Architectural decisions must be captured in ADR documents.
- Temporary discovery notes should be merged or removed once official documents are complete.

---

## 3. Document Status

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Draft | Initial version prepared from discovery or architecture work |
| Under Review | Awaiting stakeholder review |
| Accepted | Approved as current architecture or business guidance |
| Implemented | Reflected in the implemented system |
| Deprecated | Retained for history but no longer active |

---

## 4. Confidence Levels

| Level | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Confirmed | Stable architecture or business rule |
| Current Design | Current implementation target; may evolve |
| Policy Candidate | Expected to change after user feedback |
| Future Consideration | Not in current scope but intentionally anticipated |

---

## 5. Required Sections for Major Documents

Major business and architecture documents should include:

- Purpose
- Scope
- Business Context
- Ownership
- Lifecycle or Workflow
- Business Rules
- Integration Impact
- Reporting Impact
- Security and Permission Considerations
- Audit Considerations
- Open Questions
- Related Documents
