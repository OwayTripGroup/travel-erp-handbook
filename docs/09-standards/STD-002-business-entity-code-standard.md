## 1. Purpose

This document defines the standard for business entity codes in Travel MidOffice.

Business codes should be readable, unique, stable, and useful for operations, finance, support, reporting, and integration traceability.

---

## 2. General Format

Recommended format:

```text
<PREFIX>-<YYMM>-<SEQUENCE>
```

Example:

```text
SO-2607-000001
INV-2607-000001
VB-2607-000001
```

---

| Entity | Prefix | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Customer | CUS | CUS-2607-000001 |

| Product | PRD | PRD-2607-000001 |
| Order | ORD | ORD-2607-000001 |
| Customer Invoice | INV | INV-2607-000001 |
| Vendor Bill | VB | VB-2607-000001 |
| Full Credit Note | FCN | FCN-2607-000001 |
| Re-Invoice | RINV | RINV-2607-000001 |
| Sync Job | SJ | SJ-260702-000001 |
| Report Job | RJ | RJ-260702-000001 |

---

## 4. Sequence Rules

- Codes should be generated by the system.
- Codes should not be manually editable by normal users.
- Codes should remain stable after creation.
- Database IDs may be used as sequence inputs but should not be exposed alone as business numbers.
- Sequence length should be padded for readability.

---

## 5. Date Component

For business documents, use YYMM when monthly grouping is useful.

For jobs or operational logs, YYMMDD may be used.

Examples:

```text
ORD-2607-000123
SJ-260702-000123
```
